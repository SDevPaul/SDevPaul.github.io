Fudgy Chocolate Brownie Cookies

Fudgy Chocolate Brownie Cookies are very chocolaty, soft, chewy, highly fudgy, and gooey on the inside, with a crispy/crunchy exterior shell. The wonderful thing about these cookies is that they just take one bowl of cupboard ingredients and a few simple steps, making them the tastiest fudgy chocolate brownie cookies. There is no need to melt chocolate, prepare it, or use unusual ingredients.

ORIGINAL RECIPE BY KARINA CARREL, CAFE DELITES

  • Prep: 10mins
  • Cook: 12mins
  • Servings: 16 cookies

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 cup white granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 egg
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 1/3 cups all-purpose/plain flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup minimum, semi sweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 175°C. Line two baking sheets or trays with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the cocoa powder, white sugar, butter, and vegetable oil. Beat the egg and vanilla until well combined.
  3. Add the flour, baking powder, and salt; whisk the dry ingredients first, then mix them into the wet components until a dough forms. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  4. Scoop out 1-2 of dough with a cookie scoop or small icecream scoop, then place it on the baking sheets. Press them into your desired thickness.
  5. Bake in preheated oven for 10-12 minutes. The cookies must come out soft from the over and hardened over time. Do not overbake as they will dry out.
  6. Let it cooldown for 10 minutes before transferring to wire racks to further cool the cookies.
